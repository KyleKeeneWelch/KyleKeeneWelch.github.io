Biography

My name is Kyle Keene-Welch and I am currently 20 years old studying at The University Of Bedfordshire in Luton. I am a junior in my field who is developing their skills in Web Development and Software Engineering with hopes to secure a professional position at a reputable technical firm.

I grew up locally within Bedford where I had a successful education at Bedford Academy and Bedford College. The transition to higher education came from my aspirations to further my learning and secure a degree at an accredited institution. Since the transition, I have learnt and developed on several skills and concepts relating to a wide range of technical subjects with personal emphasis on those I wish to pursue in the future.

Alongside my academic development, I also had development in other areas where I was able to progress personally. This involved building confidence in areas such as a job where my experience working at Tesco provided the responsibilities that pushed me to become an effective worker, building professional and casual relationships between academic figures, professionals in the same network and meeting new friends in a new location, and building resilience and grit to overcome obstacles such as meeting deadlines, consistency at the gym and learning to drive.

I am passionate in providing a useful, effective service in the support I provide and I apply myself in everything I do, ensuring I am not only a successful professional but also a powerful team member as part of a team working towards a goal. I want to continually learn and adapt to the changing environment I place myself in and by enhancing my skills and experience in a range of projects, I can meet this demmand and shape myself to become an effective practitioner in a technical field.

Hobbies and Interests

Gym


Going to the gym as a hobby is part of my lifestyle which came about due to the personal need to work on my fitness and improve my confidence. The driving factor that enabled me to push myself to go and work on my fitness was due to the negative impacts of isolation after contracting Covid 19 back when it was prevalent. I felt really negative about my health after isolating for over a week and knew I wanted to do something about it so after confirming I was covid free, I sought to create a personal fitness plan and perform some research.

This eventually led to building upon several areas and meeting fitness objectives of which I constantly updated to the point where I am now. Nowadays, I aim to go five days a week and implement two rest days spread about the week and follow a popular push, pull, legs plan.


Casual Sports


Much like the need to improve my health with the gym, performing some casual sports also helped to meet my fitness goals. Sports such as Football, Basketball and the occasional Badminton would enable me to build my cardiovascular endurance as well as enjoying a popular pastime with some friends.

The University Of Bedfordshire provides a Get Active program which sponsers students and provides them with an account by which they can book hour sessions of various different sports and activities. Through using this service provided freely as part of my degree, I was able to attend a pre-booked Football, Basketball or Badminton session with friends at a local fitness centre and in my case, this happened to be the same place I attend the gym.


Video Games


Video games are a great way to spend your time when on a recharge after doing some work or as a social activity with some friends. Depending on the game, you can receive several different benefits such as feelings of excitement or thrill, accomplishment and satisfaction, problem solving skills and creativity, and social interaction and cooperation.

I have played a variety of different types of games reaping from many of the benefits much from my childhood and many from the present. Games on services such as the Xbox Game Pass enable me to pay for a subscription to access a catalogue of downloadable games that can be played endlessly as long as the subscription remains active. This means I can pick and choose the kind of experience I want depending on how I feel at the end of the day and I don't have to worry about paying a large expense to try something new.
